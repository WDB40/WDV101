Caring for Alaskan Dogs

Please check out our About Our Dogs section to understand some general information about our dogs before looking into how to care for them.

Type of Home

We've separated out the Do's and Don'ts of Alaskan dog homes.

Do

  • Live on a colder climate (like, you know, Alaska)
  • Live in your own home
  • Have a large yard

Don't

  • Live near the equator.
  • Live in a tiny apartment
  • Live in someone else's home (it's inevitable that the pup will eat something)

Activities

These dogs can be very active dogs, so burning as much of their energy as possible is definitely something that you want to consider.

Below are some activities that you should regularly do with your dog in order to keep him happy (and sane):

  • Daily walks
  • Trip to the dog park on weekends
  • Play with them and their toys in the house
  • Take them with you when you run
  • Hikes

As a note, do not leave them in a cage for more than a couple hours. This could lead to some bad results.

Grooming

A lot of people know about these dogs because of their beautiful fur. You will need to do a bit of maintenance to keep it in the best condition and to keep your dog health.

We recommend the standard of brushing the dog at least once or twice a week to keep all of that old fur off of them. If the build of old hair becomes too much, it can overheat the dog.

In addition to that standard, there will be two times during the year where they will heavily shed their fur. During this time, you will need to brush a little more than normal to keep your dog comfortable, happy, and healthy.

How Did We Get Here?

Grown Far from Its Roots

While the cacao tree, where we get these beans, is native to the Amazon Basin in South America. Today, nearly 70% of the beans are grown in West Africa.

Older Than the Roman Empire

There is evidence in pottery at an archaeology site in Puerto Escondido in Honduras that suggests that products made of cocoa beans were first produced and consumed between 1500 and 1400 BC. This means that despite the popularity of the Hershey chocolate bar, it probably wasn't the first chocolate bar made.

Leche con Chocolate

Even though in 1400 BC, the people of Honduras were enjoying their chocolate, it didn't become popular in Europe until the mid-17th century after it was introduced to Europe by the Spaniards. And they weren't shoveling it down as chocolate bars. During this time, they preferred their chocolate as a beverage.
